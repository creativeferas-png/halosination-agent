# HALO Agent 02 — Meeting Policy (Reference)

> Public-domain placeholder used by HALO Agent 02 (Productivity) for
> retrieval-grounded meeting summarisation. Not a confidential or proprietary
> document. Modelled on common Chief-of-Staff / PMO meeting-hygiene standards.

## Rule 1 — Decisions must be explicit
Every decision captured in meeting notes must be stated as a complete sentence
including the decision itself and its rationale. Decisions without rationale
are fragile — they get re-litigated. If a decision was made but the rationale
was not discussed, the notes should mark it as "decision recorded, rationale
to be confirmed."

## Rule 2 — Action items require named owners and deadlines
Every action item must have (a) a specific named owner and (b) an explicit
deadline. Vague phrasing like "the team will look into this" or "we'll get
back to it soon" is forbidden. If the meeting did not assign an owner, the
note must surface this as an open question rather than leaving it ambiguous.

## Rule 3 — Distinguish discussed vs. decided vs. deferred
Meeting summaries must clearly separate three categories: topics that were
discussed but not concluded, topics that resulted in concrete decisions, and
topics that were deferred to a future meeting. Conflating these creates
false consensus and leads to surprised stakeholders later.

## Rule 4 — Capture risks and blockers separately from action items
A risk is something that might block progress; an action item is something
someone will do. Risks must be logged with: the risk description, the
function or person it affects, and whether it has an owner monitoring it.
Mixing risks into the action items list buries them.

## Rule 5 — Open questions are first-class outputs
If an important question arose in the meeting and was not answered, it must
appear in the notes as an open question — never silently dropped. Each open
question should be tagged with who is best placed to answer it.

## Rule 6 — Attendees, date, and meeting purpose are mandatory metadata
Every set of meeting notes must record: the meeting date, the attendees
present (named, not roles), and a one-sentence statement of why the meeting
was held. Notes without this context are unusable for anyone who did not
attend.

## Rule 7 — Use plain, specific language
Meeting notes must use plain language, not jargon or buzzwords. Specific
nouns beat abstract verbs: prefer "ship the API endpoint" over "drive
delivery momentum." If a participant used jargon, restate it in plain terms
in the notes so non-attendees understand.

## Rule 8 — Sensitive topics get flagged
If the meeting touched on confidential, regulatory, HR, legal, or otherwise
sensitive matters, the notes must flag this and recommend a restricted
distribution. Default to wide circulation only when no sensitive flag is
present.

## Rule 9 — Follow-ups must be timestamped
Any "we'll follow up on this" item must have a concrete next-touchpoint:
either a specific date, a specific meeting at which it will be revisited,
or a specific trigger that will resurface it. "Soon" and "next week" are
not acceptable.

## Rule 10 — Notes must be readable in under 90 seconds
The summary section at the top of meeting notes must be readable in under
90 seconds. If a senior leader only reads the top section, they should
walk away knowing: what was decided, who is doing what, and what
remains open. Detailed discussion notes can follow underneath.
