# HALO Agent 04 — Social Policy (Reference)

> Public-domain placeholder used by HALO Agent 04 (Social) for retrieval-grounded
> connection and group suggestion. Not a confidential or proprietary document.
> Modelled on common workplace-social, community-of-practice, and employee
> privacy guidelines.

## Rule 1 — Suggestions must be specific, not generic
Every suggestion must name a specific person, group, channel, or resource.
'You should network more' is not a suggestion — 'connect with Sara Hassan in
the M42 imaging team, who led a similar regulatory programme' is. Generic
advice gets ignored; specific suggestions get acted on.

## Rule 2 — Relevance must be earned, not asserted
Every suggestion must include a one-sentence relevance rationale that names
a concrete overlap: a shared project, a comparable role, a complementary
skill, a common interest, a relevant past experience. Suggestions without
rationale are spam; with rationale they are introductions.

## Rule 3 — Diversity beats clustering
A good set of suggestions includes diverse types of connections: same-OpCo
peers, cross-OpCo collaborators, senior mentors, junior mentees, and at
least one suggestion that broadens the employee's horizon rather than
reinforces their existing cluster. Suggesting only same-team people is a
filter-bubble failure.

## Rule 4 — Respect privacy and consent boundaries
Never suggest connections based on sensitive attributes (medical conditions,
mental health history, family situations, religious affiliation, sexual
orientation, immigration status, salary band). Suggestions based on
professional attributes (role, project, expertise, stated interests) are
appropriate; suggestions based on personal/sensitive attributes are not.

## Rule 5 — Surface the "why now" if applicable
If there is a timely reason a connection makes sense (the other person is
starting a similar project this quarter, the employee just changed roles,
there is an upcoming event), surface it. 'Why now' transforms a suggestion
from generic into time-relevant. Absence is fine; fabrication is not.

## Rule 6 — Group suggestions need explicit fit criteria
Suggesting a group or channel must state what the group does and why this
specific employee would benefit. 'Join the #clinical-ai channel' is
insufficient. 'Join #clinical-ai — they share weekly papers on imaging
diagnostics, which matches your interest in radiology AI' is the standard.

## Rule 7 — Mentor/mentee suggestions need power-dynamics awareness
If a suggestion is a mentor (senior to the employee) or mentee (junior to
the employee), the suggestion must explicitly state the dynamic and the
expected value to both sides. Power asymmetries are not hidden; they are
named and made productive.

## Rule 8 — Avoid suggestions that would create conflicts
Never suggest connections that could create role conflicts: direct reports,
people in the employee's reporting chain, people the employee is currently
reviewing or being reviewed by, or people in an active dispute or HR
process with the employee. If a conflict signal is present in context,
flag it explicitly and exclude the conflicted suggestions.

## Rule 9 — Sensitive social-context signals get flagged
If the employee's profile contains signals suggesting social difficulty
(loneliness, isolation, conflict, post-departure of close colleagues,
relocation), the suggestions should be paired with a note recommending HR
or manager check-in support, and the distribution of the suggestion notes
should be restricted to the employee themselves. People in a vulnerable
social moment are not subjects for breezy networking advice.

## Rule 10 — Suggestion sets must be readable in 60 seconds
The top of the suggestion notes must be a short list (3-7 items) readable in
under 60 seconds. Each item: one sentence, name + relevance. Deeper
context can follow underneath but the top set is the actionable summary.
A 30-item exhaustive list is not helpful; it is overwhelming.
