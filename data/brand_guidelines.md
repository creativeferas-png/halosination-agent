# G42 Group — Brand & Communication Guidelines (Reference)

> Public-domain placeholder used by HALOsination for retrieval-grounded
> drafting. Not a confidential or proprietary document. Loosely modelled
> on common enterprise-technology brand patterns.

## Rule 1 — Voice and tone
G42 communications are confident, restrained, and technology-forward.
Avoid sales-bro vocabulary (crush, dominate, disrupt, revolutionize, game-changing,
killer, next-level). Prefer specific outcomes over superlatives. Speak as a
trusted enterprise partner, not as a startup pitch deck.

## Rule 2 — Healthcare audiences (M42 specifically)
For M42 and any healthcare-adjacent audience, language must be evidence-aware,
non-sensational, and trust-building. Do not use battle metaphors (fight, defeat,
beat the disease) in patient-facing or clinical-facing communications. Avoid
unfounded efficacy claims. Use professional, dignified language even in
emotionally weighty contexts (pediatrics, oncology, end-of-life).

## Rule 3 — Visual identity (palette)
Primary palette is technology-forward and restrained: deep navy (#001F3F),
mid blue (#005C97), accent cyan (#00A8E8), and off-white (#F4F7FB). Warm
tones (orange, yellow, red) are used sparingly and only for accent or
contrast — never as a primary palette for clinical or enterprise communications.

## Rule 4 — Visual identity (composition)
Compositions favour generous whitespace, clear hierarchy, sans-serif typography,
and abstract or data-driven imagery. Avoid clip-art, cartoon styles, comic-book
visuals, or neon/maximalist treatments. Photography, when used, should be
documentary or editorial in style, never staged or saccharine.

## Rule 5 — Healthcare imagery
Do not depict patients in distress, invasive procedures, blood, needles, or
operating-theatre scenes. Do not stage children, families, or patients in
ways that read as marketing emotion-mining. When in doubt, use abstract or
data-network imagery over patient photography.

## Rule 6 — Mandatory inclusions
Every external-facing G42-group asset must include the relevant OpCo logo
with sufficient clear space, and adhere to the legal disclaimer requirements
of the OpCo. Internal assets may use the G42 master logo only.

## Rule 7 — Forbidden words
Forbidden in any external communication: "revolutionary", "world-class" (overused),
"best-in-class" (overused), "synergy" (jargon), "leverage" as a verb,
"crush" / "dominate" / "kill" (combative), "literally" (when not literal).

## Rule 8 — Audience register
Match register to audience: enterprise tech buyers expect confident-precise tone;
hospital CIOs and clinical leaders expect evidence-aware, trust-building tone;
the public expects clear-accessible tone with minimal jargon; G42 internal
audiences (engineering, ops) may receive slightly more direct/technical tone.

## Rule 9 — Calls to action
Calls to action are direct and specific. "Learn more" is acceptable only when
no more specific action exists. Prefer "Read the case study", "Book a clinical
demo", "Speak to our team", "Download the whitepaper". Never use clickbait
phrasing ("You won't believe...", "This changes everything...").
